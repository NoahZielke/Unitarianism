Deductions from Scripture Against Jesus Being God

This article is composed of a series of if-then statements, each aimed to be a simple Biblical argument against Jesus being God. They are intentionally short, and concise, and therefore serve as a good starting point for those studying the topic of what the Bible teaches about God and Jesus, and hopefully serve as inspiration for deeper reflection and study.

Deductions

  • If the Father is the "only true God" (John 17:3), then Jesus is not God.
  • If the "one God" is the Father, explicitly in distinction from Jesus (1 Timothy 2:5: 1 Corinthians 8:5-6, Ephesians 4:4-6), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus is the Son of God (Matthew 16:13-17, John 20:31), then He is not God.
  • If Jesus is seated at the right hand of God (Psalm 110:1, Matthew 22:41-45), then He is not God.
  • If Jesus has a God (Psalm 45:6-7, Micah 5:2-4, Matthew 27:46, Mark 15:34, John 20:17, Romans 15:6: 2 Corinthians 1:3, 11:31, Ephesians 1:3, 1:17: 1 Peter 1:3, Hebrews 1:8-9, Revelation 1:5-6, 3:2, 3:12, 5:10), then He is not God.
  • If Jesus denied coming to do His own will, and instead said that He came to do the will of the Father (John 5:30, 5:43, 6:38, 8:42), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus denied speaking from Himself, and instead said that He spoke what God told Him to speak (John 7:16-18, 8:28, 12:49-50, 14:10), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus glorifying Himself is invalid, but the Father glorifying Him is valid (John 8:54), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus testifying of Himself is invalid, but the Father's testimony of Him is valid (John 5:31-37, 8:17-18), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus said that He was a "man" who told the truth which He heard from God (John 8:40), then Jesus is not God.
  • If the Father is greater than Jesus (John 14:28), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus can do nothing of Himself (John 5:19, 5:30), then Jesus is not God.
  • If God will not judge, but has committed judgement to Jesus (John 5:22), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus prayed to God (Luke 5:16, 6:12), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus is God's "servant" (Matthew 12:17-18), then Jesus is not God.
  • If speaking against Jesus is forgivable, but speaking against the Spirit of God is not (Matthew 12:31-32, Mark 3:28-29, Luke 12:10), then Jesus is not God.
  • If God alone, in distinction from Jesus, is good in an underived or ultimate sense (Matthew 19:17, Mark 10:18, Luke 18:19), then Jesus is not God.
  • If sitting at Jesus's right hand and left hand is not His to give (Matthew 20:23, Mark 10:40), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus did not know the day or the hour of His return, but God did know the day and the hour (Matthew 24:36, Mark 13:32), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus's will was not to be crucified, but He submitted His will to God's (Matthew 26:39, Mark 14:36, Luke 22:42), then Jesus is not God.
  • If God will "give" to Jesus the throne of David (Luke 1:32), then Jesus is not God.
  • If God "gave to the Son also to have life in himself" (John 5:26), then Jesus is not God.
  • If God "gave (Jesus) authority to execute judgment" (John 5:27), then Jesus is not God.
  • If all things have been "handed over" to Jesus from God (Matthew 11:27, Luke 10:22), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus was "given" all power in Heaven and on earth (Matthew 28:18), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus was preached by the Apostles as "a man approved of God" (Acts 2:22), then Jesus is not God.
  • If the Apostles said that God "made" Jesus "Lord and Christ" (Acts 2:36), then Jesus is not God.
  • If the Apostles said of Jesus that "God was with him" (Acts 10:38-42), then Jesus is not God.
  • If the Apostles described Jesus as a "man who (God) has appointed" (Acts 17:31), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus belongs to God (1 Corinthians 3:23), then Jesus is not God.
  • If "the head of Christ is God" (1 Corinthians 11:3), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus will be subject to God forever (1 Corinthians 15:27-28), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus is a the mediator between God and sinful mankind (Romans 8:34: 1 Timothy 2:5, Hebrews 7:25: 1 John 2:1), then Jesus is not God.
  • If the Father is "above all" (Ephesians 4:6), then Jesus is not God.
  • If the Father is the "only God" (1 Timothy 1:17, John 5:44, 17:3), then Jesus is not God.
  • If God, in distinction from Jesus, "gives life to all things" (1 Timothy 6:13), then Jesus is not God.
  • If the Father "alone has immortality" (1 Timothy 6:16), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus is described as being "faithful to (God) who appointed him", then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus offered Himself to God (Hebrews 9:14), then Jesus is not God.
  • If Jesus appears "in the presence of God on our behalf" (Hebrews 9:24), then Jesus is not God.
